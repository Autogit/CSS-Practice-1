SCOTT DRICKEY

IS A NEBRASKA NATIVE PHOTOGRAPHER.

His work embodies a genuine appreciation of stories – from inanimate objects to colorful personalities. He is a twenty-year seasoned professional with a strict technical background that began at the Brooks Institute in Santa Barbara, CA. This training propelled him into the start of a storied career, working alongside some of the world’s most notable photographers as an on-camera assistant and lighting gaffer across the globe.

Considering himself a late bloomer, he states, “My early assisting career may have actually hindered my ability to establish a point of view, which I feel like I just recently discovered. It’s pretty humbling to capture and tell stories for a living today. I choose to leave the tech secondary, which allows me to only develop a relationship with my subject, whether it’s an object or a person. I enjoy listening to people’s stories and having the chance to share in an experience with them. It’s those experiences that enable me to create an image or series of images that honors their true character. If I’m asked over for dinner, it’s been a success for me.”
